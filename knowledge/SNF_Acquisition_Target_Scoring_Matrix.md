# SNF Acquisition Target Scoring Matrix
## Strategic Acquisition Target Evaluation Framework

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This comprehensive scoring matrix provides a systematic approach to evaluate and prioritize skilled nursing facility and assisted living acquisition targets. The framework incorporates strategic fit, financial attractiveness, operational excellence, and market positioning factors to generate actionable acquisition intelligence.

**Matrix Scoring Range**: 1-10 scale for each category (10 = most attractive)
**Overall Score Calculation**: Weighted average across all categories
**Decision Framework**: Tier 1 (8.0+), Tier 2 (6.0-7.9), Tier 3 (4.0-5.9), Pass (<4.0)

---

## SCORING FRAMEWORK OVERVIEW

### **Category Weights:**
- **Strategic Fit (30%)**: Geographic alignment, portfolio enhancement, market position
- **Financial Performance (25%)**: Revenue, profitability, cash flow, growth potential
- **Operational Excellence (20%)**: Quality metrics, efficiency, management strength
- **Market Dynamics (15%)**: Growth prospects, competitive position, regulatory environment
- **Transaction Feasibility (10%)**: Owner motivation, valuation reasonableness, execution risk

---

## 1. STRATEGIC FIT ANALYSIS (Weight: 30%)

### **Geographic Alignment (Sub-weight: 40%)**

| Score | Criteria | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **10** | **Perfect Market Fit** | Primary target market, dense clustering opportunity, market leadership potential |
| **8-9** | **Strong Market Fit** | Secondary target market, good clustering opportunity, significant market share gain |
| **6-7** | **Moderate Market Fit** | Acceptable market, limited clustering benefit, modest market position improvement |
| **4-5** | **Weak Market Fit** | Non-target market, isolated asset, limited strategic value |
| **1-3** | **Poor Market Fit** | Undesirable market, regulatory challenges, declining demographics |

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Distance from existing operations (clustering opportunities)
- Market demographics (65+ population growth, income levels)
- Regulatory environment (certificate of need, reimbursement rates)
- Competitive landscape (market concentration, growth barriers)
- Strategic market importance (regional hub potential, referral network access)

### **Portfolio Enhancement (Sub-weight: 35%)**

| Score | Criteria | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **10** | **Transformative Addition** | Platform acquisition, new market entry, significant scale benefits |
| **8-9** | **High Strategic Value** | Market leadership position, specialty capabilities, key referral sources |
| **6-7** | **Moderate Value Add** | Market share expansion, operational synergies, service line enhancement |
| **4-5** | **Limited Enhancement** | Incremental addition, minimal synergies, basic expansion |
| **1-3** | **No Clear Benefit** | Redundant capacity, limited strategic value, integration challenges |

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Scale impact (bed count addition, revenue contribution)
- Service line diversification (SNF, ALF, memory care, rehab)
- Operational synergies (management systems, vendor contracts, best practices)
- Referral network integration (hospital relationships, physician alignment)
- Technology and innovation capabilities

### **Competitive Positioning (Sub-weight: 25%)**

| Score | Criteria | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **10** | **Market Dominance** | #1 market position, significant competitive moats, pricing power |
| **8-9** | **Strong Position** | Top 3 market position, established reputation, referral advantages |
| **6-7** | **Competitive Position** | Top 5 market position, adequate market presence, stable relationships |
| **4-5** | **Weak Position** | Lower tier position, limited competitive advantages, market share pressure |
| **1-3** | **Poor Position** | Marginal player, competitive disadvantages, market share erosion |

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Market rank by bed count and revenue
- Quality reputation and brand strength
- Physician and hospital relationships
- Payer contract advantages
- Competitive differentiation factors

## 2. FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS (Weight: 25%)

### **Revenue Profile (Sub-weight: 30%)**

| Score | Criteria | Financial Metrics |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| **10** | **Exceptional Revenue** | >$150 revenue/bed/day, >95% occupancy, premium payer mix |
| **8-9** | **Strong Revenue** | $125-150 revenue/bed/day, 90-95% occupancy, good payer mix |
| **6-7** | **Average Revenue** | $100-125 revenue/bed/day, 80-90% occupancy, mixed payer mix |
| **4-5** | **Below Average** | $80-100 revenue/bed/day, 70-80% occupancy, heavy Medicaid |
| **1-3** | **Poor Revenue** | <$80 revenue/bed/day, <70% occupancy, payer mix challenges |

**Key Metrics:**
- Average daily rate (ADR) by payer type
- Occupancy rate trends (3-year average)
- Payer mix (Medicare, Medicaid, private pay percentages)
- Revenue growth rate (3-year CAGR)
- Seasonal occupancy stability

### **Profitability Analysis (Sub-weight: 35%)**

| Score | Criteria | Financial Metrics |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| **10** | **Excellent Margins** | >20% EBITDA margin, >8% net margin, strong cash flow |
| **8-9** | **Good Margins** | 15-20% EBITDA, 5-8% net margin, positive cash flow |
| **6-7** | **Average Margins** | 10-15% EBITDA, 2-5% net margin, adequate cash flow |
| **4-5** | **Weak Margins** | 5-10% EBITDA, 0-2% net margin, limited cash flow |
| **1-3** | **Poor Margins** | <5% EBITDA, negative net margin, cash flow challenges |

**Key Metrics:**
- EBITDA margin and dollar amount
- Net income margin and trends
- Operating cash flow generation
- Working capital management
- Cost structure efficiency

### **Financial Strength (Sub-weight: 20%)**

| Score | Criteria | Financial Condition |
|-------|----------|---------------------|
| **10** | **Very Strong** | Minimal debt, strong liquidity, excellent credit profile |
| **8-9** | **Strong** | Conservative debt levels, adequate liquidity, good credit |
| **6-7** | **Adequate** | Moderate debt, sufficient liquidity, acceptable credit |
| **4-5** | **Weak** | High debt levels, limited liquidity, credit concerns |
| **1-3** | **Poor** | Excessive debt, liquidity crisis, distressed situation |

**Key Metrics:**
- Debt-to-equity ratio
- Interest coverage ratio
- Current ratio and quick ratio
- Days cash on hand
- Capital expenditure requirements

### **Growth Potential (Sub-weight: 15%)**

| Score | Criteria | Growth Profile |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| **10** | **High Growth** | Expanding market, new services, acquisition opportunities |
| **8-9** | **Good Growth** | Growing market share, service expansion, operational improvements |
| **6-7** | **Moderate Growth** | Stable market position, selective expansion, efficiency gains |
| **4-5** | **Limited Growth** | Mature market, minimal expansion, maintenance mode |
| **1-3** | **Declining** | Market share loss, service reduction, defensive position |

**Key Metrics:**
- Historical revenue growth (3-year CAGR)
- Market share trends
- Expansion pipeline (beds, services, markets)
- Capital investment plans
- Demographic growth in service area

## SCORING MATRIX CALCULATION

### **Overall Score Formula:**
```
Overall Score = (Strategic Fit × 0.30) + (Financial Performance × 0.25) + 
               (Operational Excellence × 0.20) + (Market Dynamics × 0.15) + 
               (Transaction Feasibility × 0.10)
```

### **Decision Framework:**

#### **Tier 1 Strategic Targets (Score: 8.0-10.0)**
- **Action**: Immediate pursuit and active engagement
- **Priority**: Highest priority for acquisition discussions
- **Resources**: Full due diligence team assignment
- **Timeline**: Aggressive pursuit with priority LOI submission

#### **Tier 2 Opportunistic Targets (Score: 6.0-7.9)**
- **Action**: Active monitoring and relationship building
- **Priority**: Secondary priority for acquisition consideration
- **Resources**: Preliminary due diligence and market research
- **Timeline**: Patient approach with relationship development

#### **Tier 3 Platform Targets (Score: 4.0-5.9)**
- **Action**: Passive monitoring and periodic evaluation
- **Priority**: Future consideration based on strategic changes
- **Resources**: Basic intelligence gathering and contact maintenance
- **Timeline**: Long-term relationship building without immediate pursuit

#### **Pass Targets (Score: <4.0)**
- **Action**: No active pursuit unless fundamental changes occur
- **Priority**: Intelligence monitoring only
- **Resources**: Minimal resource allocation
- **Timeline**: Re-evaluate annually or based on material changes

## EXAMPLE TARGET EVALUATION

### **Case Study: Regional Family Operator - Southeast Market**

#### **Target Profile:**
- **Company**: Regional Healthcare Partners (fictional example)
- **Portfolio**: 12 facilities, 1,450 beds across Georgia and South Carolina
- **Ownership**: Family-owned, 2nd generation, founder retiring
- **Financial**: $65M annual revenue, 16% EBITDA margin, moderate debt

#### **Scoring Analysis:**

##### **Strategic Fit: 8.5/10**
- Geographic Alignment: 9/10 (Perfect fit with expansion strategy)
- Portfolio Enhancement: 8/10 (Regional platform, quality facilities)
- Competitive Positioning: 8/10 (Market leader in 3 counties)

##### **Financial Performance: 7.2/10**
- Revenue Profile: 7/10 ($122/bed/day, 88% occupancy)
- Profitability: 8/10 (16% EBITDA, 6% net margin)
- Financial Strength: 7/10 (Moderate debt, good liquidity)
- Growth Potential: 7/10 (Stable growth, expansion opportunities)

##### **Operational Excellence: 8.0/10**
- Quality Metrics: 8/10 (4-star average, minimal deficiencies)
- Operational Efficiency: 8/10 (Good productivity, modern systems)
- Management Quality: 8/10 (Experienced team, succession planning)

##### **Market Dynamics: 8.5/10**
- Growth Prospects: 9/10 (Exceptional demographics, Atlanta suburbs)
- Competitive Landscape: 8/10 (Favorable competition, barriers to entry)
- Regulatory Environment: 8/10 (Supportive state policies)

##### **Transaction Feasibility: 9.0/10**
- Owner Motivation: 9/10 (Retirement planning, family transition)
- Valuation Reasonableness: 9/10 (7.5x EBITDA, market rates)
- Execution Risk: 9/10 (Clean structure, experienced advisors)

#### **Overall Score Calculation:**
```
Overall Score = (8.5 × 0.30) + (7.2 × 0.25) + (8.0 × 0.20) + (8.5 × 0.15) + (9.0 × 0.10)
              = 2.55 + 1.80 + 1.60 + 1.28 + 0.90
              = 8.13/10
```

#### **Strategic Recommendation: TIER 1 STRATEGIC TARGET**
- **Priority**: Highest priority for immediate pursuit
- **Rationale**: Exceptional strategic fit, strong financials, motivated seller
- **Next Steps**: Executive outreach, relationship building, LOI preparation
- **Timeline**: Target LOI submission within 60 days

## IMPLEMENTATION GUIDELINES

### **Scoring Process:**
1. **Data Collection**: Gather comprehensive information across all categories
2. **Team Evaluation**: Multi-disciplinary team scoring (operations, finance, strategy)
3. **Calibration**: Regular scoring calibration sessions for consistency
4. **Documentation**: Detailed rationale for all scores and assumptions
5. **Review Cycle**: Quarterly score updates based on new information

### **Quality Controls:**
- **Multiple Reviewers**: Require 2+ team members to score each target
- **Variance Analysis**: Flag and reconcile significant scoring differences
- **Benchmark Validation**: Regular validation against completed transactions
- **External Input**: Industry advisor input on complex evaluations
- **Audit Trail**: Maintain detailed scoring history and assumption changes

### **Decision Support:**
- **Executive Dashboard**: Real-time scoring and ranking visualization
- **Scenario Analysis**: Sensitivity testing for key assumptions
- **Comparative Analysis**: Side-by-side target comparisons
- **Pipeline Management**: Integration with CRM and deal tracking systems
- **Performance Tracking**: Post-acquisition validation of scoring accuracy
