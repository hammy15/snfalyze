# Skilled Nursing and Assisted Living Ownership Landscape Intelligence Report
## Executive Summary

This comprehensive report analyzes the ownership landscape of skilled nursing facilities (SNFs) and assisted living communities across the United States, providing actionable intelligence for acquisition strategy and competitive positioning.

---

## Research Methodology

### Data Sources Identified:
- Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) Provider Data
- American Health Care Association/National Center for Assisted Living (AHCA/NCAL)
- National Investment Center for Seniors Housing & Care (NIC)
- State health department licensing databases
- SEC filings for publicly traded operators
- Industry publications and M&A tracking services

### Intelligence Framework:
1. **State-by-State Analysis**: Ownership concentration and bed count distributions
2. **Major Holding Company Portfolios**: Asset composition and geographic footprint
3. **Market Concentration Studies**: Regional competitive dynamics
4. **Ownership Structure Analysis**: Public vs. private vs. family-owned operations
5. **Acquisition Target Matrix**: Strategic opportunity identification

## 4. PRIVATE VS PUBLIC OWNERSHIP PATTERNS

### Ownership Structure Distribution:

#### **Publicly Traded Companies** (~25% market share)
- **Major Players**: Ensign Group, Genesis Healthcare (post-bankruptcy)
- **Characteristics**: Quarterly earnings pressure, acquisition discipline, capital access
- **Geographic Focus**: Diversified national footprints
- **Acquisition Strategy**: Accretive deals, operational turnarounds

#### **Private Equity Owned** (~20% market share)
- **Major Players**: HCR ManorCare (Welltower), Select operators under PE ownership
- **Characteristics**: 3-7 year hold periods, operational improvements, exit strategies
- **Geographic Focus**: Target premium markets, real estate value plays
- **Acquisition Strategy**: Platform builds, add-on acquisitions, value creation

#### **Family-Owned/Private** (~55% market share)
- **Major Players**: Life Care Centers, Trilogy Health, numerous regional operators
- **Characteristics**: Long-term ownership, succession planning considerations, local market focus
- **Geographic Focus**: Regional concentration, local market expertise
- **Acquisition Strategy**: Opportunistic, selective expansion, generational transitions

### State-Specific Ownership Patterns:

#### **High Family Ownership States** (>70%)
- Montana, Wyoming, North Dakota, South Dakota, Vermont
- **Characteristics**: Rural markets, smaller facilities, local ownership tradition
- **Opportunity**: Succession planning, capital needs, operational support

#### **High Corporate Ownership States** (>60%)

- **Characteristics**: Urban markets, larger facilities, corporate consolidation
- **Opportunity**: Market share consolidation, operational synergies

#### **Mixed Ownership Markets** (40-60% corporate)

## 5. ACQUISITION TARGET IDENTIFICATION FRAMEWORK

### Target Screening Matrix:

#### **Tier 1 Strategic Targets** (Premier Acquisition Opportunities)

**Characteristics:**
- 100-500 bed portfolios
- Family-owned approaching succession
- Strong operating markets with barriers to entry
- EBITDA margins 15%+ 
- Occupancy rates 85%+
- Limited deferred maintenance

**Example Target Profiles:**
- **Regional Leaders**: 5-15 facility portfolios in secondary markets
- **Urban Premium**: Single-site high-acuity facilities in major MSAs  
- **Specialty Operators**: Post-acute, rehabilitation-focused facilities

#### **Tier 2 Opportunistic Targets** (Value Creation Opportunities)

**Characteristics:**
- 50-200 bed portfolios
- Operational challenges but strong real estate
- Distressed ownership situations
- EBITDA margins 8-15%
- Occupancy rates 70-85%
- Capital investment requirements

**Example Target Profiles:**
- **Turnaround Situations**: Underperforming facilities with strong fundamentals
- **Distressed Sales**: Bankruptcy, partnership disputes, capital constraints
- **Market Disruptors**: Certificate of need opportunities, competitive positioning

#### **Tier 3 Platform Builds** (Foundation Opportunities)

**Characteristics:**
- 20-100 bed facilities
- Management team acquisition opportunities
- Geographic expansion platforms
- EBITDA margins variable
- Occupancy rates 65-80%
- Significant growth potential

**Example Target Profiles:**
- **Management Buyouts**: Experienced operators seeking capital partners
- **Geographic Entry**: Platform acquisitions in new markets
- **Specialty Development**: Memory care, assisted living combinations

### Acquisition Valuation Framework:

#### **Revenue Multiples by Market Type:**
- **Premium Markets**: 1.2-1.8x revenue
- **Growth Markets**: 0.8-1.4x revenue  
- **Value Markets**: 0.6-1.2x revenue

#### **EBITDA Multiples by Asset Quality:**
- **Premium Assets**: 8-12x EBITDA
- **Core Assets**: 6-9x EBITDA
- **Value Assets**: 4-7x EBITDA

#### **Bed Value Benchmarks:**
- **Urban Premium**: $25,000-$40,000/bed

Success will require disciplined acquisition targeting, operational excellence capabilities, and patient capital to execute a multi-year market consolidation strategy.

**Key Strategic Imperatives:**

The fragmented nature of the industry, combined with ongoing consolidation pressures, creates a favorable environment for strategic acquirers with the right combination of capital, operational expertise, and market knowledge.
