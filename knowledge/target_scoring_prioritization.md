# Target Scoring & Prioritization Systems

## Multi-Dimensional Scoring Framework

### Core Evaluation Dimensions

#### Strategic Fit Assessment (40% Total Weight)

##### 1. Market Position Strength (15% weight)
**Scoring Criteria (1-5 scale)**:
- **Market Leadership (5 points)**: Dominant market share (>25%), strong brand recognition, price-setting capability
- **Strong Position (4 points)**: Significant market share (15-25%), recognized brand, competitive pricing power
- **Competitive Position (3 points)**: Moderate market share (8-15%), established presence, price follower
- **Niche Player (2 points)**: Small market share (<8%), specialized focus, limited pricing power
- **Weak Position (1 point)**: Minimal market share (<3%), struggling brand, price pressures

**Evaluation Factors**:
- [ ] Market share trend (growing, stable, declining)
- [ ] Competitive moat strength and sustainability
- [ ] Brand recognition and customer loyalty metrics
- [ ] Pricing power and margin resilience
- [ ] Regulatory barriers to competition
- [ ] Network effects and switching costs

##### 2. Strategic Rationale (15% weight)
**Scoring Criteria (1-5 scale)**:
- **Transformational (5 points)**: Fundamentally enhances core strategy, creates new business model
- **Highly Strategic (4 points)**: Significantly accelerates strategic objectives, major capability addition
- **Strategic (3 points)**: Advances key strategic initiatives, meaningful capability enhancement
- **Tactical (2 points)**: Supports existing strategy, incremental improvement
- **Opportunistic (1 point)**: Limited strategic value, purely financial transaction

**Evaluation Factors**:
- [ ] Core competency enhancement potential
- [ ] Geographic expansion value and market access
- [ ] Product/service portfolio complementarity
- [ ] Customer base expansion and cross-selling opportunity
- [ ] Technology and innovation capability addition
- [ ] Supply chain and operational synergy potential

##### 3. Synergy Potential (10% weight)
**Scoring Criteria (1-5 scale)**:
- **Exceptional (5 points)**: >20% of combined revenue in identified synergies
- **High (4 points)**: 15-20% of combined revenue in synergies
- **Moderate (3 points)**: 10-15% of combined revenue in synergies
- **Low (2 points)**: 5-10% of combined revenue in synergies
- **Minimal (1 point)**: <5% of combined revenue in synergies

**Synergy Categories**:
- [ ] Revenue synergies (cross-selling, market expansion, pricing)
- [ ] Cost synergies (operational, procurement, overhead)
- [ ] Technology synergies (platform consolidation, innovation)
- [ ] Tax synergies (structure optimization, jurisdiction benefits)

#### Financial Attractiveness (35% Total Weight)

##### 1. Valuation Appeal (20% weight)
**Scoring Criteria (1-5 scale)**:
- **Exceptional Value (5 points)**: >30% discount to intrinsic value, compelling entry multiple
- **Strong Value (4 points)**: 20-30% discount to intrinsic value, attractive multiple
- **Fair Value (3 points)**: 10-20% discount to intrinsic value, reasonable multiple
- **Full Value (2 points)**: 0-10% discount to intrinsic value, market multiple
- **Overvalued (1 point)**: Premium to intrinsic value, stretched multiple

**Valuation Metrics**:
- [ ] EV/Revenue multiple vs. comparables
- [ ] EV/EBITDA multiple vs. benchmarks
- [ ] DCF upside potential and sensitivity analysis
- [ ] Precedent transaction multiple comparison
- [ ] Sum-of-the-parts valuation assessment
- [ ] Real options value recognition

##### 2. Financial Quality (15% weight)
**Scoring Criteria (1-5 scale)**:
- **Excellent (5 points)**: High-quality recurring revenue, strong margins, robust cash generation
- **Good (4 points)**: Stable revenue streams, healthy margins, consistent cash flow
- **Average (3 points)**: Mixed revenue quality, acceptable margins, adequate cash flow
- **Below Average (2 points)**: Volatile revenue, pressured margins, inconsistent cash flow
- **Poor (1 point)**: Declining revenue quality, weak margins, poor cash generation

**Financial Quality Indicators**:
- [ ] Revenue predictability and recurring revenue percentage
- [ ] Margin trends and sustainability analysis
- [ ] Cash flow quality and conversion rates
- [ ] Balance sheet strength and leverage profile
- [ ] Working capital efficiency and management
- [ ] Return on invested capital trends

#### Execution Feasibility (25% Total Weight)

##### 1. Integration Complexity (15% weight)
**Scoring Criteria (1-5 scale)**:
- **Very Low (5 points)**: Minimal integration requirements, compatible systems/culture
- **Low (4 points)**: Straightforward integration, manageable complexity
- **Moderate (3 points)**: Standard integration challenges, proven solutions available
- **High (2 points)**: Complex integration requirements, significant resource needs
- **Very High (1 point)**: Extremely complex integration, high failure risk

**Complexity Factors**:
- [ ] Cultural compatibility and alignment assessment
- [ ] Technology and systems integration requirements
- [ ] Organizational structure and process harmonization
- [ ] Geographic integration challenges and requirements
- [ ] Regulatory and compliance integration complexity
- [ ] Customer and supplier integration considerations

##### 2. Deal Execution Risk (10% weight)
**Scoring Criteria (1-5 scale)**:
- **Very Low (5 points)**: Cooperative management, clear path to close, minimal regulatory risk
- **Low (4 points)**: Willing seller, straightforward process, low regulatory hurdles
- **Moderate (3 points)**: Standard deal process, manageable regulatory requirements
- **High (2 points)**: Complex negotiations, significant regulatory challenges
- **Very High (1 point)**: Hostile situation, major regulatory obstacles, financing uncertainty

**Execution Risk Factors**:
- [ ] Management team cooperation and alignment
- [ ] Due diligence access and transparency
- [ ] Regulatory approval probability and timeline
- [ ] Financing certainty and structure flexibility
- [ ] Competitive bidding dynamics and position
- [ ] Timeline constraints and closing conditions

## Advanced Scoring Models

### Risk-Adjusted Scoring Framework

#### Risk Weighting Methodology
**Risk Categories and Impact**:
- **Market Risk (25% impact)**: Industry decline, competitive threats, regulatory changes
- **Execution Risk (25% impact)**: Integration complexity, management retention, synergy realization
- **Financial Risk (20% impact)**: Leverage levels, cash flow volatility, working capital needs
- **Strategic Risk (15% impact)**: Fit with portfolio, capability gaps, technology disruption
- **Operational Risk (15% impact)**: Key person dependencies, system reliability, process maturity

#### Risk-Adjusted Score Calculation
```
Base Score = Weighted Average of Dimension Scores
Risk Multiplier = 1 - (Composite Risk Score × Risk Factor)
Final Score = Base Score × Risk Multiplier

Where Risk Factor = 0.3 (maximum 30% score reduction for highest risk)
```

## Target Prioritization Matrix

### Tier Classification System

#### Tier 1: Immediate Pursuit (Score 4.0-5.0)
**Resource Allocation**:
- [ ] Dedicated senior management sponsorship
- [ ] Primary corporate development team assignment
- [ ] Expedited due diligence process (4-6 weeks)
- [ ] External advisor engagement authorization
- [ ] Board-level approval for competitive bidding

**Action Items**:
- [ ] Initial management presentation within 2 weeks
- [ ] Preliminary valuation and financing structure
- [ ] Competitive intelligence gathering and analysis
- [ ] Regulatory approval pathway assessment
- [ ] Integration planning initiation

#### Tier 2: Strategic Consideration (Score 3.0-3.9)
**Resource Allocation**:
- [ ] Department head sponsorship and oversight
- [ ] Secondary team assignment with periodic review
- [ ] Standard due diligence timeline (8-12 weeks)
- [ ] Selective external advisor support
- [ ] Management committee approval for pursuit

**Action Items**:
- [ ] Management presentation within 4-6 weeks
- [ ] High-level valuation range development
- [ ] Market intelligence gathering
- [ ] Preliminary integration complexity assessment
- [ ] Strategic fit validation studies

#### Tier 3: Opportunistic Monitoring (Score 2.0-2.9)
**Resource Allocation**:
- [ ] Analyst-level coverage and monitoring
- [ ] Minimal resource commitment
- [ ] Basic due diligence only if approached
- [ ] No external advisor engagement
- [ ] Department-level approval authority

**Action Items**:
- [ ] Quarterly review and score updates
- [ ] Environmental scanning and market monitoring
- [ ] Relationship maintenance with target management
- [ ] Value enhancement opportunity identification
- [ ] Market condition change sensitivity analysis

#### Tier 4: Pass/Future Consideration (Score <2.0)
**Resource Allocation**:
- [ ] Passive monitoring only
- [ ] No active resource commitment
- [ ] Annual review and reassessment
- [ ] No current management engagement
- [ ] Documentation for future reference

**Action Items**:
- [ ] Annual scoring model updates and reassessment
- [ ] Industry and competitive landscape monitoring
- [ ] Strategic rationale evolution tracking
- [ ] Market condition and valuation changes
- [ ] Technology or business model disruption signals
