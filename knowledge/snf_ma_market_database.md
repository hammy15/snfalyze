## Comprehensive Analysis 2022-2024

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The skilled nursing facility (SNF) M&A market has demonstrated resilient transaction activity despite headwinds from rising interest rates, labor cost inflation, and regulatory uncertainty. Total transaction volume for 2022-2024 period estimated at $12-15 billion across 1,200+ facilities, with notable geographic concentration in high-growth Sunbelt markets and defensive positioning in established Northeast corridors.

**Key Market Findings:**
- Cap rates range from 5.5% (premium gateway cities) to 10.0% (secondary/rural markets)
- Per-bed valuations span $35K (Midwest secondary) to $180K (California premium)
- Private equity remains dominant buyer category (40% of capital deployed)
- Sale-leaseback structures account for 35% of transaction volume
- Technology-enabled facilities command 15-25% valuation premiums

## 5. SALE-LEASEBACK TRANSACTION ANALYSIS

### TRANSACTION STRUCTURE OVERVIEW

Sale-leaseback transactions represent approximately 35% of SNF transaction volume, providing operators with capital access while maintaining operational control. These structures have become increasingly popular as operators seek to:

- **Access Growth Capital:** Fund expansion without dilutive equity raises
- **Optimize Balance Sheets:** Convert real estate assets to operational liquidity  
- **Maintain Control:** Retain operational management and resident relationships
- **Benefit from Real Estate Appreciation:** Participate in upside through renewal options

### TYPICAL LEASE TERMS

**Lease Duration:** 15-25 years initial term
**Renewal Options:** 3-5 five-year extensions  
**Rent Escalations:** 2.0% - 3.5% annual increases
**Tenant Responsibilities:** Triple-net lease structure (taxes, insurance, maintenance)
**Assignment Rights:** Limited transferability, operator approval required

### MAJOR SALE-LEASEBACK PARTICIPANTS

#### **Buyers (Real Estate Investors):**
- Healthcare REITs seeking stable income streams
- Private equity funds with long-term investment horizons
- Insurance companies requiring duration matching
- Pension funds seeking inflation-protected cash flows

#### **Sellers/Lessees (Operating Companies):**
- Multi-facility operators seeking growth capital
- Family-owned operators planning succession
- Regional platforms requiring balance sheet optimization
- Distressed operators needing liquidity injection

### MARKET OPPORTUNITIES & TRENDS

**Current Market Conditions:**
- Cap rates typically 50-100 bps higher than direct sales
- Strong institutional buyer demand for credit-rated lessees
- Favorable financing environment for real estate buyers
- Operational performance improvement opportunities

**Emerging Trends:**
- **Master Lease Structures:** Portfolio-level lease arrangements
- **Performance-Based Rent:** Occupancy or revenue participation
- **Development Partnerships:** Joint venture development projects
- **Technology Integration:** Smart building systems as lease provisions

## 7. DEAL STRUCTURE & FINANCING TRENDS

### TRANSACTION STRUCTURE EVOLUTION

#### **Asset vs. Stock Purchases**
- **Asset Purchases:** 75% of transactions (liability limitation preference)
- **Stock Purchases:** 25% of transactions (operational continuity priority)
- **Hybrid Structures:** Emerging for complex multi-state portfolios

#### **Portfolio vs. Single-Asset Transactions**
- **Single Assets:** 60% of deal count, 35% of dollar volume
- **Portfolio Transactions:** 40% of deal count, 65% of dollar volume  
- **Portfolio Premium:** 15-25% pricing benefit for scale transactions

### FINANCING LANDSCAPE ANALYSIS

#### **Debt Capital Sources & Terms**

**Commercial Banks (35% of financing)**
- **Typical LTV:** 70-75%
- **Interest Rates:** Prime + 150-300 bps
- **Amortization:** 25-30 years
- **DSCR Requirements:** 1.25x minimum
- **Advantages:** Relationship-based, flexible terms
- **Disadvantages:** Portfolio concentration limits, regulatory oversight

**Life Insurance Companies (25% of financing)**  
- **Typical LTV:** 65-75%
- **Interest Rates:** 10-year Treasury + 200-350 bps
- **Amortization:** 25-30 years  
- **DSCR Requirements:** 1.35x minimum
- **Advantages:** Long-term fixed rates, patient capital
- **Disadvantages:** Strict underwriting, limited flexibility

**CMBS Market (20% of financing)**
- **Typical LTV:** 70-80%
- **Interest Rates:** Swap + 250-400 bps
- **Amortization:** 25-30 years
- **DSCR Requirements:** 1.20x minimum  
- **Advantages:** Competitive pricing, standardized process
- **Disadvantages:** Limited flexibility, market timing dependency

**Private Debt Funds (15% of financing)**
- **Typical LTV:** 75-85%
- **Interest Rates:** SOFR + 400-700 bps
- **Amortization:** Interest-only to 10 years
- **DSCR Requirements:** 1.15x minimum
- **Advantages:** Speed, flexibility, complex situations
- **Disadvantages:** Higher cost, shorter terms

**FHA/HUD Programs (5% of financing)**
- **Typical LTV:** 85-90%
- **Interest Rates:** Treasury + 200-300 bps  
- **Amortization:** 35-40 years
- **DSCR Requirements:** 1.20x minimum
- **Advantages:** High leverage, long amortization
- **Disadvantages:** Regulatory complexity, processing time

#### **Market Conditions Impact on Financing**

**Interest Rate Environment (Current):**
- 10-Year Treasury: 4.25-4.75% range
- Credit Spreads: Stable, moderate risk premiums
- Forward Curve: Gradual decline anticipated 2025-2026
- Fed Policy: Data-dependent, measured approach

**Credit Market Dynamics:**
- **Lender Appetite:** Selective, quality-focused
- **Competition:** Moderate among established relationships
- **Terms:** Covenant packages tightening selectively
- **Pricing:** Stable with modest risk-adjusted increases
