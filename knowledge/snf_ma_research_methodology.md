### B. Per-Bed Valuation Analysis by Region

#### Northeast Corridor ($75K - $150K per bed)
- Highest land costs and regulatory barriers
- Premium facilities command top valuations
- Sale-leaseback structures common

#### Southeast ($40K - $90K per bed)  
- Growth demographics drive investor interest
- Lower operating costs vs. Northeast
- Certificate of Need regulatory considerations

#### Midwest ($35K - $75K per bed)
- Value-oriented market segment
- Rural/urban pricing disparities
- Reimbursement pressure impacts

#### Southwest ($45K - $95K per bed)
- Texas and Arizona growth markets
- Retirement migration patterns
- Energy sector economic correlation

#### West Coast ($80K - $180K per bed)
- California regulatory complexity
- Premium market positioning
- Supply constraints drive values

### C. Deal Volume & Pricing Trends (2022-2024)

#### 2022 Market Conditions
- Post-COVID recovery phase
- Elevated cap rates due to interest rate uncertainty
- Quality focus over volume

#### 2023 Market Dynamics  
- Interest rate stabilization

- Regional performance divergence

#### 2024 Current Environment
- Demographic growth acceleration

## Deal Structure & Financing Analysis

### Common Transaction Structures
1. **Asset Purchase** - Direct facility acquisition
2. **Sale-Leaseback** - Operating company retains operations
3. **Portfolio Transactions** - Multi-facility packages
4. **Joint Ventures** - Development partnerships
5. **Management Contracts** - Fee-based structures

### Financing Landscape
**Debt Sources:**
- Commercial banks (relationship-driven)
- Life insurance companies (long-term holds)  
- CMBS market (standardized properties)
- Private debt funds (bridge/transitional)
- FHA-backed financing (owner-operators)

**Typical Terms:**
- LTV ratios: 65-80%
- Amortization: 25-30 years
- Interest rates: Prime + 150-400 bps
- Debt service coverage: 1.25x minimum
