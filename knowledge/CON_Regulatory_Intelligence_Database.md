## State-Specific Application Processes {#application-processes}

### Tier 1 States (Most Complex/Restrictive)

#### New York (Comprehensive Model)
- **Agency**: NYS Department of Health
- **System**: NYSE-CON Electronic System
- **Review Types**: 
  - Administrative Review (minor projects)
  - Limited Review (moderate projects) 
  - Full Review (major projects)
- **Key Triggers**: 
  - New facility establishment
  - Construction/renovation >$15M
  - Ownership changes
  - Service additions/deletions
  - Major medical equipment
- **Documentation**: 40+ schedule types required
- **Public Process**: Required for Full Reviews
- **Timeline**: 6-18 months typical
- **Fee Structure**: Tiered based on project cost
- **Recent Updates**: Title 10 amendment (August 2025) - new construction parameters

#### Florida (Market-Based Approach)
- **Agency**: Agency for Health Care Administration (AHCA)
- **System**: FloridaHealthFinder portal
- **Review Focus**: 
  - Need methodology with market analysis
  - Financial feasibility requirements
  - Quality metrics assessment
- **Approval Criteria**: 
  - Community need demonstration
  - Financial capability
  - Quality performance history
- **Timeline**: 4-12 months
- **Unique Features**: 
  - Gold Seal recognition program
  - Comprehensive financial dashboards
  - Real-time facility monitoring

#### Maryland (Integrated Health Planning)
- **Agency**: Maryland Health Care Commission
- **Focus Areas**: 
  - Comprehensive Health Planning
  - Capital expenditure thresholds
  - Service area analysis
- **Integration**: Links CON with rate-setting and quality oversight
- **Timeline**: 6-15 months

### Tier 2 States (Moderate Complexity)

#### Virginia (Streamlined Process)
- **Recent Updates**: 2024 ownership transfer simplification
- **Focus**: Cost containment and access assurance
- **Timeline**: 4-10 months

#### North Carolina (Active Reform Environment)
- **Status**: Multiple reform bills in 2023 (HB 259, HB 76, SB 115)
- **Trend**: Toward deregulation in specific areas
- **Timeline**: 3-8 months

#### Georgia (Selective Requirements)
- **Recent Changes**: 2024 psychiatric facility exemptions
- **Focus**: Hospital and nursing home oversight
- **Timeline**: 3-6 months

### Tier 3 States (Simplified Process)

#### Connecticut (Pilot-Friendly)
- **Recent Changes**: Exemptions for harm reduction centers
- **Approach**: Targeted regulation
- **Timeline**: 2-6 months

#### South Carolina (Nursing Home Focus)
- **Status**: 2023 partial repeal - only nursing homes retained
- **Scope**: Highly targeted
- **Timeline**: 2-4 months
